My journey into web programming

Into the web

It has taken me 10 years to embrace my love of web development and dedicate myself to the craft. I studied computer science but instead of taking up programming my career path lead me into the role of Systems Analyst.

Rather than move into Project Management I've changed course to application development. Most definitely an unconventional choice, but I'd like to concentrate on projects that somehow have a positive impact.

One step at a time

The journey has been like a roller coaster ride. Filled with anxiety when approaching new hurdle, absolute rapture as the understanding of a technology crystallized and devastating lows when staggering through minefields of realised misconceptions.

Now that my formal training is complete, I can't wait to apply the knowledge I've amassed over the past months, but also to keep learning and growing as a part of the programming community.

Programming community

Many programmers in Sydney generously share their time and knowledge to encourage newbie programmers.

Rails Girls is my favourite event and the monthly Ruby On Rails DevHub and Ruby On Rails Oceania meetups are excellent opportunities to meet experience programmers.
